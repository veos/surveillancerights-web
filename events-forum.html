Public Forum: Video Surveillance in Canada

Tuesday June 4, 2013, 1 - 4pm

Room 728, Bissell Bldg.
140 St. George Street,
University of Toronto

When you walk around the city, you are being WATCHED.
You are being SORTED.
What do YOU know about your privacy rights?
How should Canadian video surveillance policy develop in the future and who should have a voice in the process?

Do you, as Canadians, care about the privacy implications of current video information practices and their implications for individual visual privacy?

A public forum on Video Surveillance Practices and Policy in Canada will engage people in a broader discussion about video surveillance issues. The forum will feature a new position paper on Surveillance by the Information and Privacy Commissioner of Ontario, Canada, Dr. Ann Cavoukian and a panel discussion which will include representation from government, industry, civil society, and academe.

This public forum is part of the larger research project, “Who is Watching You? And Why?” funded by the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.

For more information, please contact Joseph Ferenbok (joseph.ferenbok [at] utoronto.ca). You may download a PDF of this announcement here.

Itinerary

1:00pm - Welcome: Introduction to project and primer for discussion

1:30pm - IPC Announcement: Dr. Ann Cavoukian, Information and Privacy Commissioner of Ontario, Canada, will present the latest IPC paper on surveillance and privacy.

2:00pm - Panel Discussion: Moderated by Professor Andrew Clement, with panelists:

  • Professor Lisa Austin (Faculty of Law, University of Toronto)
  • Dr. Ann Cavoukian (Information and Privacy Commissioner, Ontario, Canada)
  • Abby Deshman (Director, Public Safety Program, Canadian Civil Liberties Association)
  • John Wunderlich (Privacy and Security Consultant)
  • Representative from Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (TBA)

3:45pm - Closing Remarks
