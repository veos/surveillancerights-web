Fieldwork

What we did:

We talked to 126 people in public spaces that were subject to video surveillance, and asked them interview questions grouped into categories including general awareness of video surveillance; legal uses; privacy rights and knowledge; perceptions and information needs about surveillance; infrastructure knowledge and legitimacy of video surveillance.

We developed many questions under each of the categories, and asked them in a rotating sequence, including:

  • Do you know this area is under video surveillance?
  • What do you think the organisation conducting this surveillance does with it?
  • What do you think would be the most important features to know about video surveillance?
  • Should law enforcement officers be required to get a warrant to access video surveillance collected by cameras they do not operate themselves?
  • How would you like to be notified about camera locations? and
  • Does knowing this area is under video surveillance make you feel more or less safe?
  • Have you heard anything about facial recognition or video analytics?

Highlights:

  • Most participants knew they were being surveilled.
  • Most assumed the purpose of the surveillance was for “security,” but were divided on the issue of whether it made them feel more or less safe, and most weren’t sure exactly what organisations might do with surveillance data.
  • Very few knew about privacy regulations relating to video surveillance, although many assumed that such regulations probably did or should exist.
  • Many thought police should require a warrant to access video surveillance conducted by private-sector operators.
  • The majority wanted signs to tell them about surveillance taking place; small signs in non-obvious locations were criticized by some participants as inadequate.
  • Many had heard of the concept of facial recognition although they often weren’t sure exactly how it worked, but almost none knew anything about video analytics.
  • Most participants thought it was inappropriate to use video surveillance data for individual profiling.
